## 1. Introducing Your Design System with a Resonating Brand Name

### Brand Name: **Riff**

**Justification:**
"Riff" is a short, punchy, and memorable name that draws directly from the language of music‚Äîparticularly guitar playing. A riff is a repeated chord progression or refrain in music that forms the backbone of a song. Similarly, this design system forms the consistent, reusable foundation upon which Fender Play interfaces are built. The name evokes creativity, rhythm, and continuity‚Äîkey traits in both music education and great UI design. Riff captures the essence of Fender‚Äôs brand while being approachable and developer-friendly.

### ‚úçÔ∏è Content Guidelines

#### Voice and Tone

* **Voice**: Friendly, supportive, motivational.
* **Tone**: Clear, confident, warm.

**It is:**

* Encouraging: "You‚Äôre doing great!"
* Clear: "Next, try this chord."
* Positive: "You‚Äôve unlocked your next lesson."

**It is not:**

* Sarcastic: "Really? That‚Äôs the best you can do?"
* Overly casual: "Yo, let‚Äôs shred some chords, dude."

#### Copywriting Guidelines

* Keep copy concise, actionable.
* Use active voice.
* Avoid jargon.
* Highlight benefits (CTAs like "Start Practicing").
* Sentence case except for proper nouns.

#### Imagery & Illustrations

* **Style**: Clean, modern, Fender-aligned.
* **Usage**:

  * Reinforce learning moments.
  * Enhance onboarding.
  * Support empty states.
* **Principles**:

  * No clutter.
  * Represent diverse learners.

#### Elevation & Hierarchy

* Use shadow, size, spacing for hierarchy.
* Elevate interactive elements.
* Use type scale and color contrast for readability.
* Group content with cards and containers.
